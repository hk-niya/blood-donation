Are You Eligible to Donate?

Most healthy adults can donate blood. General guidelines include:

  • Be in good general health and feeling well.
  • Be at least 17 years old (or 16 with parental consent in some areas).
  • Weigh at least 110 lbs.
  • Have not donated whole blood in the last 56 days (8 weeks).

Check Your Donation Interval

If you've donated before, ensure at least 56 days have passed since your last whole blood donation.

For specific eligibility questions, please consult with donation center staff or your healthcare provider.

The Blood Donation Process: Step by Step

Understanding the donation process can help you feel more prepared and comfortable. Here‚Äôs what you can typically expect:

1

Registration

Upon arrival, you'll complete a confidential registration form with your personal details and sign a consent form. Please bring a valid photo ID.

2

Health Screening

A trained staff member will conduct a private and confidential interview about your health history and lifestyle. They'll also check your temperature, pulse, blood pressure, and hemoglobin level (a small prick on your finger).

3

The Donation

You'll proceed to a donor couch. A phlebotomist will clean an area on your arm and insert a new, sterile needle for the blood draw. The actual donation takes about 8-10 minutes for one unit (about a pint) of blood.

4

Refreshments & Rest

After donating, you'll rest for 10-15 minutes and enjoy some complimentary refreshments. This helps your body adjust and replenish fluids.

5

After Care

Drink extra fluids for the next 24-48 hours. Avoid strenuous physical activity or heavy lifting for the rest of the day. If you feel dizzy, lie down until the feeling passes. Contact the donation center if you have any concerns.
